Read More About Our Partners

World Health Organisation, or WHO, categorizes as a United Nations Specialised Agency and is the coordinating authority on international health within the United Nations system. WHO always adheres to the UN values of integrity, professionalism, and respect for diversity. The WHO is a globally covering organization with clear goals to selflessly defend the individual's rights to good and sustainable health. The organization also works closely with coordinating responses to health emergencies such as natural disasters, water scarcity, sickness but also supplies technical assistance to countries in need.

World Wildlife Foundation The work that the WWF does sometimes takes them to the most difficult places on Earth, those communities often face debilitating levels of violence, poverty, and weak applications of societal law. The World Wildlife Foundation works tirelessly to preserve the natural resources so that these places can find a pathway to development and stability. Supporting individuals in need of resources to fund their families is a paramount part of the work WWF does. And prioritizing community support and engagement has helped many communities from water shortages, soil erosion, and disaster relief.

Wildlife Conservation Network protects endangered wildlife by supporting the conservationists who ensure wildlife and people coexist and thrive. With their network of united supporters and staff all around the world, they help maintain a balance of environmental awareness that makes humans and animals co-exist on Earth. There is regularly guest speakers from other collaborating organization attending their seminars, showing their extensive reach. WCN regularly awards scholarships to promising young conservationists carrying on the ideas for future generations.

World Water Council seeks to position water at the top of the global political agenda, and produce worldwide policies to develop, authorize and manage water resources. The council aims to reach the whole global political sphere. covering national governments, parliamentarians and local authorities as well as United Nations bodies. WWC tackles many issues facing water security and seeks to challenge convention and create new ways to think about it as a force for change. Together with its investors and members, the council explores areas where it sees the potential to increase the visibility of water and its challenges.

National Geographic Is not the traditional world health organization, but contributes to spread information to the masses trough easily accessible mediums in stunning photography, showing issues that is relevant to the sustainability of our planet, pollution, scarcity, and natural disasters. Although photographers working for National Geographic does seldom do environmental health work, the information that the company has about the issues as seen by real human eyes and not researched by satellites or only in numbers are unparalleled.

Circle of Blue is a nonprofit organization that aims to meet the demand for information and collaboration in their challenge, to fight the water crisis. Founded in 2000 by journalists and scientists. Uniting classical journalism, data literacy and transformative connectivity, and providing on-the-ground information about the world's resource crisis with a focus on water. Awarded not only a Seal of Transparency by GuideStar. But also a Rockefeller Centennial Innovation Award, one of the United States top honors.
